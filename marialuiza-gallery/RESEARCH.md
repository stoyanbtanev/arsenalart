## History & Background

### The Bar (Klub Maria Luiza / "Маратa")
- One of the oldest bars in Plovdiv, operating since **1993** (~30+ years)
- Founded as a family business by **Boyan Sopadzhiev (father)** with his two sons **Boyan Jr** and **Zhorj (George)**, and their wives
- Located between two centuries-old temples and ancient basilicas at the foot of Plovdiv's **Old Town**
- Named after **Princess Maria Luiza** (sister of Tsar Simeon II) — the family wrote to Tsar Simeon for permission, received a letter of approval
- Known by locals as **"Маратa" (Marata)**
- The underground floor opened 2 years after the bar (1995), adapted for club/dance setting with jazz dances
- Featured in **The European Bar Guide** (inscribed 2022): described as "Stylish, bawdy, artsy corner dance hall"
- Venue Type: Corner Bar / Café Bar / Dance Hall / Compact / Art Space

### The Gallery
- **Founded in 2020** as a natural extension of the bar
- Purpose: to present the art of both **rising and established artists**
- Located in the **ground-level space** of Club Maria Luiza
- The walls have always displayed paintings from the family's personal art collection, collected over the years from artist friends
- Historically a gathering place for famous Plovdiv artists: **Yoan Leviev, Georgi Bozhilov-Slona**, and others
- Part of Plovdiv's **art bohemia** for nearly 30 years
- The gallery creates an **informal atmosphere** with friendly artist-audience interaction

### Key People
- **Boyan Sopadzhiev (father)** — founder of the bar
- **Boyan Sopadzhiev (son)** — current gallerist/operator, inherited the business
- **Zhorj Sopadzhiev** — artist, co-owner; grandson and protégé of the great Bulgarian painter **Paskal Struzhev**; some paintings on the walls are his own work
- **Pantelei Popov (Panti)** — chef, a NATFA graduate, creates artisanal food (duck liver confit, burritos, homemade chips)
- **Yoko** — the Jack Russell terrier, mascot of the bar (named after Yoko Ono)

## Cultural Significance

- A multi-generational gathering place — several generations of Plovdiv artists have passed through
- Has been a favorite of famous Plovdiv artists and family friends of the Sopadzhiev family
- Younger artists like **Kolyo Karamfilov** also frequented the bar
- Most clients have "historical memory and nostalgia for the Plovdiv of the past"
- Hosts annual **boutique jazz concerts** for **NOŠ Plovdiv** festival (Night of Plovdiv) — live jazz in front of the entrance, under the stars
- Recognized by **plovdiv.bg** (official Plovdiv municipality website) for hosting exhibitions
- Featured in multiple Plovdiv cultural media: **Plovdiv Online, Pod Tepeto, Lost in Plovdiv, BNR Radio Plovdiv, Marica newspaper**
- Listed on **TripAdvisor** as a bar/restaurant

## Recent Exhibitions (2024–2026)

### 2026
1. **"Настроени" (Tuned)** — Даниела Стоянова & Никола Певичаров (March 13, 2026)
   - Joint exhibition exploring formal similarities: color spots, delicate halftones, rhythmic and harmonic composition
   - Poetic metaphor, softened symbolism
   
2. **"Фрагменти от виденията" (Fragments of Visions)** — Жорж Сопаджиев (February 20, 2026)
   - 28 works: 11 mosaics, 12 oil paintings, 5 watercolors
   - A deeply artistic project by the grandson of Paskal Struzhev

3. **"Отвъд предела" (Beyond the Limit)** — Любен Хранов memorial (February 2026)
   - "Abstract Mysticism" 1979–2025
   - Memorial exhibition for the Plovdiv artist who suddenly passed away at end of 2025

### 2025
4. **"Точно време" (Exact Time)** — Венета Маринова (January 31, 2025)
   - Solo exhibition by the Plovdiv painter on the eve of her birthday
   - Works in painting, collage, wood sculpture, performance, video art
   - Ran until February 5, 2025

5. **AMTII Photography Exhibition** (September 12, 2025)
   - Students from Academy of Music, Dance and Fine Arts "Prof. Asen Diamandiev"
   - Curated by Dr. Sonya Stankova
   - Participants: Gaya Vardeva, Georgi Doychev, Georgi Lukov, and others

### 2024
6. **"Включване" (Inclusion)** — 20 artists (October 7, 2024)
   - 31 years after graduating from Plovdiv Art Gymnasium "Tsanko Lavrenov"
   - Diverse art forms: painting, graphics, illustration, photography, digital art, agar art
   - Curated by Zlatka Shetkova

### Opening Exhibition (2020)
7. **Opening Exhibition** (October 2, 2020)
   - 12 artists including Zhorj Sopadzhiev, Stanimir Videv, Nikola Pevicharov, Vladimir Chukić, Monika Romenska, Stoyan Zavarinov
   - Featured painting, sculpture, drawings, and graphics

## Atmosphere & Unique Selling Points

1. **30+ year heritage** — one of the oldest bars in Plovdiv, an artistic institution
2. **Royal connection** — named with permission from Tsar Simeon II after Princess Maria Luiza
3. **Multi-generational** — generations of Plovdiv artists, from masters to emerging talents
4. **Art + Gastronomy** — combines gallery exhibitions with artisanal bar food by a NATFA-trained chef
5. **Informal & Bohemian** — no pretense; friendly artist-audience interaction; the "art bohemia of Plovdiv"
6. **Location** — at the foot of the Old Town, between ancient temples and basilicas
7. **Live Jazz** — annual concerts for NOŠ Plovdiv festival, weekly cultural events
8. **Family-run** — authentic, personal, soulful atmosphere
9. **Diverse art forms** — from classical painting to digital art and agar art
10. **Community hub** — where marriages and divorces were created, where artists left part of their hearts
